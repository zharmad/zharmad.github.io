2DChemistry

This is a gas-phase toy model designed to help you understand chemical processes and reactions, in terms of what's going on at the atomic level.

In this flattened universe, molecules behave like hard circles that obey Newtownian laws of motion. They collide with each other and with the wall boundaries, changing direction and rotational speed as needed to imitate their counterparts in our own reality.

Whenever two reactant molecules collide in an acceptable orientation and with sufficient energy, its corresponding product molecules will then be formed. This is collision theory in a nutshell.

In addition, unstable molecules may randomly decompose if it has sufficient rotational energy to do so.

The above rules are all you need to obtain a conceptual understanding of various high school chemistry content.

Physical quantities from our universe have been copied over with one exception: the amount of energy required or transferred during reactions. These are divided by ten so that, within the tiny length and time scales of the toy model, it becomes feasible for you to observe chemical processes in one sitting.

Thus, the standard units are:

  • pico/nanometers,
  • femto/picoseconds,
  • Kelvin,
  • kilojoules,
  • atomic-mass-units, and
  • moles.
If you wish to check values in this toy model against their equivalents in our world, please note that both the energy division and the compression from 3D to 2D will implact multiple measurements. These include the average speed of gas molecules, rates of reaction, autoignition temperature, etc.

The simulation preset button at the top of this panel will get you started on a few different models.
